# Pair Discord

WednesdayAI appears in Discord as a bot in your server. There is nothing to install — just find the bot and start chatting.

## What you need

- Access to the Discord server where your administrator added the bot

## Steps

### 1. Find the bot in your server

The bot appears in the member list on the right side of your Discord server. Your administrator can tell you the bot's name.

### 2. Start a conversation

You have two ways to chat:

**In a channel:** Type your message in any channel the bot has access to. Mention the bot to get its attention:

```
@YourBotName what can you help me with?
```

**Via DM:** Click the bot's name in the member list and select **Message**. In a DM, you do not need to mention the bot — every message you send goes directly to your AI.

### 3. Read the response

Your AI will reply in the same channel or DM thread, usually within a few seconds.

## Slash commands

In Discord, you can also use slash commands. Type `/` in any channel and look for your bot's commands in the list.

Common commands:

| Command | What it does |
| ------- | ------------ |
| `/reset` | Start a fresh conversation |
| `/think` | Toggle extended thinking |
| `/model` | Switch to a different AI model |

## Tips

- **DM vs channel** — DMs are private between you and the bot. Channel messages are visible to everyone in the channel.
- **Threads** — In some server configurations, the bot replies in a thread. This keeps the channel tidy and your conversation private from other channel members.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The bot doesn't respond in a channel">
    The bot may not have permission to read or write in that channel. Ask your server administrator to check the bot's channel permissions. Alternatively, try DMing the bot directly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't DM the bot">
    Your server privacy settings may block DMs from bots. In Discord, go to **Server Settings → Privacy Settings** and enable **Allow direct messages from server members**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The bot says I'm not authorised">
    Your Discord user ID may not be in the allowed list. Ask your WednesdayAI administrator to add you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Slash commands don't appear">
    Slash commands may take a few minutes to appear after the bot is added to a server. If they're still missing after an hour, ask your administrator to check the bot's application command registration.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
