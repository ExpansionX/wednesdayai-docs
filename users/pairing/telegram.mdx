# Pair Telegram

Connecting WednesdayAI on Telegram is the simplest pairing of any channel - there is no QR code to scan. You just find the bot your administrator set up and send it a message.

## What you need

- Telegram installed on your phone or computer
- The bot username from your administrator (e.g. `@MyAIBot`)

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Get the bot username from your administrator">
    Your administrator set up a Telegram bot when configuring WednesdayAI. Ask them for the bot's username - it starts with `@` and usually ends in `Bot`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Open a chat with the bot">
    In Telegram, tap the search icon and type the bot username. Select it and tap **Start**.

    Or open this link (replace `YourBotName` with the actual username):

    ```
    https://t.me/YourBotName
    ```
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send your first message">
    Tap **Start** in the chat, or just type a message. Your AI will respond within a few seconds.

    That is it - you are connected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Native slash commands

On Telegram, WednesdayAI commands also appear as **native commands**. Type `/` in the chat and Telegram shows a menu of available commands (such as `/reset`, `/status`, and `/model`) - tap one instead of typing it out. When a command needs a choice, Telegram shows buttons to pick from.

Text commands still work too, so you can type `/status` directly if you prefer.

## Group chats

If your administrator has added the bot to a Telegram group, you can chat with your AI there too. In a group the bot stays quiet until you get its attention - either:

- Mention it by username: `@YourBotName what is the weather today?`
- Reply to one of the bot's previous messages

## Tips

- **Notifications** - make sure Telegram notifications are enabled so you see replies on your phone.
- **Multiple devices** - Telegram syncs across all your devices. You can start on your phone and continue on desktop.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The bot does not respond">
    Make sure you tapped **Start** before sending a message - Telegram requires this for bots. If you already tapped Start and there is still no reply, ask your administrator to run `openclaw doctor` to check the Telegram channel status.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I cannot find the bot">
    Double-check the username with your administrator. Bot usernames are case-insensitive but must match exactly. Try searching without the `@` symbol.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The bot says I'm not allowed">
    Your Telegram account ID may not be in the allowed users list. Ask your administrator to add you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Messages in a group are ignored">
    In group chats you need to mention the bot by username or reply to its messages. It will not respond to every message in a busy group by default.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
